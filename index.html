01 — A Moment Happening

You're walking
through a mall.

You weren't planning to stop.
But a small crowd is gathered around something that doesn't look like a store.

It looks like a photo exhibition.
People in very ordinary clothes.
Looking unexpectedly extraordinary.
Forty-eight photos.
Forty-eight outfits.
"3 minutes. 6 pieces. If you do it well, your photo goes up here."

A guide notices you looking. "You have three minutes inside. Six pieces. Wear them however you like. If it's interesting, you join the wall."

Now you're not just curious. You want to try.

You step forward without thinking twice.

We all say the same thing:

"I have nothing
to wear."

Said standing in front of a full wardrobe.

But our wardrobes are full.
We don't lack clothes.

04 — The Insight

It was never about how many clothes we own.

It was about how we were looking at them.

Newness is not in the wardrobe.

It's in the eye.

And eyes can change.

05 — The Brand Belief

Unfold exists
in this tension.

A modular clothing brand designed to be worn in multiple ways. Not to give you more clothes. But to give you more ways to see the ones you already have.

Not to give you more clothes.

But to give you more ways to see the ones you already have.

01Perspective over purchase
02Creativity over consumption
03Identity over trend
04Sustainability through use

So instead of telling people this…

We built a room where they
could discover it themselves.

— Stepping out

You step out
adjusting a sleeve.

And then you turn.

48

The same six pieces.

That's when you see The Wall properly for the first time.

Forty-eight photos. The same six pieces you just wore.
Layered. Twisted. Wrapped. Reversed. Reimagined.

You look at The Wall.

You look at yourself.

Back at The Wall.

Something feels… off.
Not wrong. Just incomplete.

— The question

The guide stands beside you. He looks at The Wall with you.

"Which one
surprised you
the most?"

You scan the photos. You point without thinking.
And you realise — you never even considered wearing it like that.

— Going back in

"Want to try again?"

Back into the booth.

Same six pieces.
Different eyes.

You pick up the piece you ignored before.

You stop trying to be safe.
You start trying to be interesting.

The mirror doesn't feel like a mirror now.
It feels like a possibility.

— The moment

You step out again.

Smiling without noticing.

A photo is taken.

Printed instantly.

Pinned onto The Wall.

Belonging.

For the first time, you notice a word on the back:

Unfold.

No one told you that. You discovered it yourself.

07 — Production

Designed to travel.
Built to prove its values.

01

Venue

Central mall atrium · High foot-traffic urban zones · 12m × 8m · 5m ceiling · Natural foot traffic eliminates media spend.

02

The Wall

Modular aluminium · 5m × 4m · Reprinted daily · Panels reused across all cities · Certified 3-city minimum reuse.

03

The Booths

Flat-pack sustainable timber · LED timer only · No screens inside · 2-day build · 4-hour strike · 2 booths simultaneously.

04

The Guides

2 per shift · No uniform · Dressed like the audience · One question only · Never sell · Never explain · Create space.

Sustainability in the production itself

All materials certified for 3-city reuse. Wall panels travel flat-packed. UNFOLD garments used in the installation go on sale after each city closes. 100% recycled print stock. If a decision can't survive the scrutiny of someone who just walked out of that booth — it doesn't belong here.

08 — What They Leave With

They don't leave talking about the installation.

They don't leave talking about the brand.

They leave talking about
what they realised.

They post their picture with a caption they were never asked to write:

"I don't need more
to feel new."

That sentence spreads.
Not because a brand said it.
Because they felt it.

And the next time they stand in front of their wardrobe…

They pause. They try differently. They see differently.
